Kurukshetra is a historical city in the northern Indian state of Haryana. It is believed to be the site of the ancient city of Kurukshetra, which was the setting for the Mahabharata, one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India. The Mahabharata tells the story of the Kuru dynasty and the great battle that took place between the Kauravas and the Pandavas, two branches of the same family. According to Hindu tradition, the battle of Kurukshetra was fought on a massive scale, involving thousands of soldiers and lasting for 18 days. The city is also considered a sacred pilgrimage site for Hindus, and is home to several temples and other religious sites.

K.C. Memorial

Kalpana Chawla Memorial Planetarium has been setup at Kurukshetra-Pehowa road by Haryana State Council for Science & Technology,Govt. of Haryana in collaboration with Council for Science Museums.

Sheikh Chilli Tomb

Main tomb belongs to Sufi Abd-ur-Rahim Abdul-Karim Abd-ur-Razak,popularly known by the name of Sheikh Chilli.He was Qadiriyya Sufi master of Mughal Prince Dara Shikoh.The architectural plan of the tomb shows Persian influence.

Brahma Sarovar

According to stories of itihasa , Lord Brahma created the universe from the land of Kurukshetra after a huge yajna with the help of Hitesh. The Brahma Sarovar here is believed to be the cradle of civilization.

Jyotisar

Jyotisar, on the bank of Jyotisar Sarovar wetland, is a Hindu pilgrimage site in the city of Kurukshetra in Haryana state of India. In mythology, it is where Krishna delivered the sermon of Bhagavad Gita

Shri Krishna Museum

The museum, unravels the mystique of the cult of Lord Krishna. It endeavours to present the multifaceted personality of Krishna as narrated in the episodes of Mahabharata and the Bhagvata Purana.

Dharohar

Dharohar, a museum to showcase the unique archeological, cultural and architectural heritage of Haryana. It also has an open-air theatre for cultural performances.It is situated at dharamnagri kurukshetra.
